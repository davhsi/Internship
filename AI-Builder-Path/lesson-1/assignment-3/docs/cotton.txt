Cotton is a soft, fluffy, natural staple fiber composed almost entirely of cellulose.
It grows in protective cases, called bolls, around the seeds of the cotton plant (genus Gossypium).
It is widely cultivated for manufacturing breathable, durable, and biodegradable textiles.
Core CharacteristicsComposition: ~87–90% pure cellulose, along with minor percentages of natural waxes, water, and pectins.
Fiber Structure: Under a microscope, fibers look like flat, twisted ribbons.
Benefits: Highly absorbent (soaking up to 24-27% of its own weight), soft, easy to dye, and machine-washable.
Main Uses
Apparel: Used globally to produce everything from lightweight voiles to heavy denim and velveteen.
Home Textiles: Commonly used for bed sheets, towels, and upholstery.
Medical & Personal Care: Used for disposable nonwoven products like bandages, cotton balls, and swabs.
